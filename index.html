Mini Academy ‚Ä¢ Step 3 Ranges 101 ‚Äî reading A2:F200 without fear.
‚åÑ

A range is just an area in your sheet.

A2:A100 = one column (A), from row 2 to row 100.
A2:F200 = a rectangle from column A to F, rows 2 to 200.
'Tasks'!A2:F200 = the same rectangle, but on a tab called ‚ÄúTasks‚Äù.

If your list is shorter, you only change the last number. For example, if your list stops at row 60, use A2:F60 instead of A2:F200.

üìÑ How to write a range from another tab (with clear examples)
When your data lives in a different tab, you simply add the tab name + an exclamation mark before the range.

Here are the most common examples:
‚Ä¢ 'Tasks'!A2:F200 ‚Üí data in a tab called Tasks.
‚Ä¢ 'My List'!C2:C60 ‚Üí a column in a tab with spaces in its name (‚ÄúMy List‚Äù).
‚Ä¢ '2025 Projects'!A2:B500 ‚Üí tables inside tabs with numbers and spaces.

‚ùó What the exclamation mark means

‚Ä¢ Go to the tab called Tasks
‚Ä¢ Then read the range A2:F200

üîÅ Extra clarity:
